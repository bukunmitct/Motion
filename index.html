MOTION

Motion, in physics, change with time of the position or orientation of a body.

Types of motion

Motion along a line or a curve is called translation.

Motion that changes the orientation of a body is called rotation.
In both cases all points in the body have the same velocity (directed speed) and the same acceleration (time rate of change of velocity). The most general kind of motion combines both translation and rotation.

Sir Isaac Newton created the laws of motion after several experiments .

Three laws are:

1. A body remain at rest or in motion at a constant speed in a straight line ,unless acted upon by an external force .example;

2. When a body is acted upon by a force ,the time rate of change of its momentum equals the force .Mathematicaly ,P=mV

3. If two bodies exert forces on each other ,these forces have the same magnitude but opposite directions
